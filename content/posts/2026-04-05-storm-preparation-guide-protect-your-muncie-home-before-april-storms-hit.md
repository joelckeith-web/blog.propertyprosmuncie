## Critical Pre-Storm Roof Inspection Checklist

Your [roof](https://www.propertyprosmuncie.com/roofing/) serves as your home's primary defense against severe weather, making pre-storm inspection essential. Before Monday's scattered rain showers intensify into this week's storm pattern, examine these critical roofing components:

**Shingle Assessment**
- Look for loose, cracked, or curling shingles that wind could easily lift
- Check for granule loss indicating advanced shingle deterioration
- Identify any missing shingles from winter weather damage
- Examine edges and corners where wind uplift typically begins

**Flashing and Sealant Inspection**
- Inspect flashing around chimneys, vents, and skylights for gaps or corrosion
- Check roof-to-wall intersections where water commonly penetrates
- Verify that all penetrations have adequate sealant without cracks
- Look for rust stains indicating compromised metal components

**Structural Elements**
- Examine fascia boards for rot or damage that could compromise gutter attachment
- Check for sagging areas indicating potential structural issues
- Verify that all roofing nails are properly seated and not backing out

Professional [roof inspections](https://www.propertyprosmuncie.com/roof-inspections/) from Property Pros Muncie can identify subtle vulnerabilities that untrained eyes might miss, potentially preventing thousands of dollars in storm damage.

> **Key Takeaway:** A comprehensive pre-storm roof inspection focuses on shingles, flashing, and structural elements. Professional assessment by Property Pros Muncie identifies vulnerabilities before severe weather exploits them, protecting your home investment.

## Gutter System Storm Preparation

With heavy rainfall forecast for Friday through Sunday, your [gutter system](https://www.propertyprosmuncie.com/gutters/) must function flawlessly to protect your home's foundation and exterior walls. Clogged or damaged gutters during heavy storms can cause water backup leading to roof damage and basement flooding.

**Essential Gutter Maintenance Steps**
- Remove all debris including leaves, twigs, and accumulated sediment
- Test water flow by running garden hose through each section
- Check for loose hangers or brackets that storm winds could compromise
- Verify that downspouts direct water at least 6 feet from foundation
- Examine joints and seams for separation that allows water escape

**Downspout and Drainage Evaluation**
- Ensure all downspout connections are secure and properly sealed
- Check that splash blocks or underground drainage systems function properly
- Clear any obstructions in underground drainage lines
- Verify that water flows away from foundation during test runs

Given the forecast's 50°F temperature swing from Tuesday's freeze to weekend storms, expansion and contraction could worsen existing gutter issues. Address minor problems now before they become major failures during heavy rainfall.

## Siding and Exterior Wall Protection

Temperature fluctuations from 29°F to 80°F create significant expansion and contraction stress on [siding materials](https://www.propertyprosmuncie.com/siding/), while subsequent storms test every sealed joint and connection point. Proper siding preparation prevents water intrusion that leads to costly interior damage.

**Pre-Storm Siding Inspection Points**
- Check for loose or damaged siding panels that wind could lift
- Examine caulking around windows, doors, and trim for cracks or gaps
- Look for holes or punctures that could allow water penetration
- Verify that all siding is properly fastened and aligned

**Window and Door Sealing**
- Test all window and door seals for air leaks indicating compromise
- Apply fresh caulk to any gaps found around frames
- Check weatherstripping condition and replace if worn
- Ensure storm doors and windows are properly installed and latched

The [Federal Emergency Management Agency](https://www.fema.gov) emphasizes that proper exterior sealing prevents 90% of storm-related water intrusion damage, making pre-storm preparation far more cost-effective than post-storm repairs.

> **Key Takeaway:** Siding and exterior wall preparation focuses on sealing gaps and securing loose materials. The extreme temperature swing forecast for Muncie makes pre-storm sealing critical to prevent water intrusion during subsequent heavy rainfall.

## Foundation and Drainage Preparation

The combination of freeze conditions followed by heavy rainfall creates unique challenges for foundation drainage systems. Frozen ground prevents proper water absorption, increasing runoff that tests foundation waterproofing and drainage capacity.

**Foundation Inspection Priorities**
- Check for new cracks that freeze-thaw cycles may have created
- Verify that foundation vents are clear and functional
- Examine basement or crawl space for existing moisture issues
- Ensure proper grading directs water away from foundation walls

**Landscape Drainage Assessment**
- Clear storm drains and catch basins of debris accumulation
- Check that yard grading promotes water flow away from structures
- Identify low spots where water might pool against foundation
- Remove any landscaping materials that could become storm debris
